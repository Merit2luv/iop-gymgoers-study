# Comparative Assessment of Resting Intraocular Pressure in Regular Gym-Goers and Non-Gym-Goers

**A Cross-Sectional Study from Ilorin Metropolis, Kwara State, Nigeria (N = 120)**

Final-year research project · Doctor of Optometry (O.D.) · Department of Optometry and Vision Science, Faculty of Life Sciences, University of Ilorin, Nigeria · July 2026

**Author:** Ofikwu Matthew Iduh
**Supervisor:** Dr. A. E. Agbabiaka

---

## Overview

This study investigates whether regular gym participation is associated with lower resting intraocular pressure (IOP) — the single most important modifiable risk factor for glaucoma, a leading cause of irreversible blindness in Nigeria and sub-Saharan Africa.

A comparative cross-sectional design was used, recruiting 120 adults in Ilorin metropolis (60 regular gym-goers, 60 non-gym-goers) via multistage sampling. Resting IOP was measured with a calibrated tonometer, and data were analyzed in SPSS v26.0 using descriptive statistics, an independent-samples t-test, effect size estimation, and bivariate correlation.

## Key Finding

Gym-goers had a **significantly lower mean resting IOP (14.82 ± 2.30 mmHg)** than non-gym-goers **(16.73 ± 3.22 mmHg)**:

- t(106.885) = −3.741, **p < .001**
- **Cohen's d = 0.68** (medium-to-large effect)
- No significant relationship between exercise *frequency* and IOP within the gym-goer group (r = .139, p = .288) — suggesting the benefit comes from being a regular exerciser at all, rather than a dose-response gradient

**Interpretation:** Regular gym participation is associated with meaningfully lower resting IOP, supporting physical activity counselling as a low-cost, scalable component of glaucoma-risk management — particularly relevant in a healthcare context like Nigeria's, where access to glaucoma medication and surgery can be limited.

## Methodology

| | |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Comparative cross-sectional study |
| **Sample** | 120 adults (60 gym-goers, 60 non-gym-goers), multistage sampling |
| **Setting** | Ilorin metropolis, Kwara State, Nigeria |
| **Measurement** | Resting IOP via calibrated tonometer (right eye, left eye, average); blood pressure, BMI, and lifestyle/medical history also recorded |
| **Analysis** | Shapiro-Wilk normality testing, independent-samples t-test (Welch-adjusted), Cohen's d, Pearson/Spearman correlation — SPSS v26.0 |

Both groups were balanced at baseline on age, blood pressure, and BMI, supporting the conclusion that the IOP difference reflects gym participation rather than pre-existing physiological differences.

## Limitations

- Single IOP measurement per participant (no diurnal variation captured)
- Gender imbalance between groups (63.3% male gym-goers vs. 63.3% female non-gym-goers), noted as a potential confounder
- Self-reported exercise frequency rather than objective activity tracking
- Cross-sectional design cannot establish causal direction
